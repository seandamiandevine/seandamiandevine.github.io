    Bio

    I am a PhD student in the Otto Lab at McGill University. My main research interests are twofold:
     
    1. the computational mechanisms underpinning effortful choice
    2. studying real-world decisions through the use of big consumer data.
     
    My current research projects include studying how non-instrumental information can motivate effortful decision-making and context can be influence large-scale human decision-making.
