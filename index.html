    Bio

    As a researcher...

    I am a PhD student in the Otto Lab at McGill University. My main research interests are twofold:
     
    1. studying how information-seeking affects effortful decision-making;
    2. studying real-world decisions through the use of big consumer data.
     
    My current research projects include studying how non-instrumental information can motivate effortful decision-making and how normalization (divisive and sequential) can be applied to understand large-scale human behaviour.
     

    As an editor...

    I am the interim-Editor-in-Chief and full-time Editor of Psychology for the Journal of Trial and Error—a journal dedicated to the discussion, critical reflection, and publication of scientific works that produce null, mixed, or unexpected results.
     
    Failure is not a bug, it's a feature of science, and we aim to give it a platform.
