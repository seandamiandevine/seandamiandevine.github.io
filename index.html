Otto Lab

Bio

    PhD student in the Otto Lab at McGill University. My research interests are twofold: 1) studying effortful decision-making with understudied incentive strcutures; 2) studying real-world decisions through the use of big consumer data.
     
    My current research projects include studying how non-instrumental information can motivate effortful decision-making and how normalization (divisive and sequential) can be applied to understand large-scale human behaviour.
     
    If you are interested in colloborating, connecting, or plain-old chatting about cognition and data, don't hesite to contact me!
